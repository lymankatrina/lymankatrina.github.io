Cat Faced Spiders in Idaho

by Katrina Lyman

The warm weather of spring awakens new flowers and many sleeping critters like the Araneus Gemmoides, commonly known as the cat-faced spider.

The cat-faced spider is mainly found outdoors, like the one pictured here, which was on my deck. They may look a bit creepy, but they are considered harmless.

Cat-faced spiders like to build their webs near lights, and are often found on the eaves of buildings and under overhangs. They have a large abdomen and are easy to identify because of their unique shape. They change color from summer to winter, ranging from pale yellow to rust colored.

When the female spider produces one large eggsac, she usually dies shortly after. In the spring, hundreds of baby spiders hatch, but many of them don't make it to adulthood, because their siblings will eat them. Those who do survive perform a great service to the world. They eat many garden insects and spiders when they are grown.
