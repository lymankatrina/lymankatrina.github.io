Mountain Lion Encounters

While crossing paths with a mountain lion is out of the ordinary, there seem to be a few enocunters with mountain lions in southeast Idaho every year. Local residents become concerned when the encounters occur close to their homes, work, or recreation areas.

Here are some safety tips to remember if you happen to encounter a mountain lion. Do not run and never turn your back on the mountain lion. Boldly stand your ground, facing the lion and making yourself appear as large and strong as possible. Raise your arms over your head and wave them. Yell or shout and throuw objects or items if the mountain lion does not leave the area. Typically mountain lions will run off. In the rare instance that a mountain lion attacks, fight back! Try and stay on your feet and use anything you can to fight back. Mountain lion attacks on humans are very rare. If you see one, you should never approach it or offer it food.

If you observe a mountain lion or signs of a mountain lion near your home or neighborhood, contact the Idaho Department of Fish and Game.
